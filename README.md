# EcoDrive-Sales-Analysis
OVERVIEW
This project analyzes business performance and sustainability metrics for EcoDrive Motors, a vehicle company specializing in Diesel, Petrol, and Electric Vehicles (EVs). The dashboard provides insights into sales performance, profitability, and environmental impact to support strategic decision-making.


KEY DASHBOARDS
KPI 1: Sales and Revenue Performance:
• Analyse sales and revenue by:
o Vehicle type and model
o Region
o Time period
o Sales representative and other relevant dimensions.
• Identify strong and weak performance areas (e.g. high-performing regions vs underperforming 
ones).

KPI 2: Profit Contribution:
• Analyse:
o Gross profit
o Net profit
o Profit margins
• Compare profitability by vehicle type and model.
• Identify the most and least profitable products and segments.

KPI 3: Environmental Sustainability Performance: • Assess sustainability performance using:
o Carbon footprint reduction
o Fuel efficiency
• Estimate emissions avoided through EV sales.
• Evaluate progress towards 2030 sustainability targets.

KEY INSIGHTS
KPI 1: Sales and Revenue Performance
-Total revenue reached 483M with 15K units sold, with Model A generating the highest revenue, followed closely by Model C and Model E, while Model D performed the weakest. Petrol vehicles showed consistently higher revenue trends over time compared to Diesel and Electric.
-Manchester recorded the highest revenue, followed by Leeds and Edinburgh, while Birmingham was the lowest-performing region. Sales performance across representatives was relatively balanced, each contributing around 19–21%, indicating no heavy reliance on a single salesperson.

KPI 2: Profit Contribution
-The business achieved a gross profit of 143M and net profit of 100M, with an overall profit margin of 21%. Petrol vehicles contributed the highest share of net profit (45%), followed by Diesel (35%), while Electric vehicles contributed the least (20%) despite their strategic importance.
-By product model, Model A generated the highest gross profit, while Model D was the least profitable, indicating that not all high-revenue products translate into strong profitability.

KPI 3: Environmental Sustainability Performance
-Electric Vehicles (EVs) showed lower profit contribution but are critical for sustainability, contributing to reduced carbon emissions and improved fuel efficiency compared to petrol and diesel vehicles.
-While progress toward 2030 carbon reduction targets is evident through EV adoption, the lower sales and profit share of EVs suggests the company needs to scale EV adoption further to fully meet sustainability goals.

RECOMMENDATIONS
KPI 1: Sales & Revenue – Recommendations
-Focus on high-performing regions (Manchester, Leeds) by increasing inventory and sales efforts, while implementing targeted marketing and promotions in Birmingham to improve low performance.
-Scale top-performing models (Model A, Model C) through strategic pricing and availability, while reviewing or repositioning low-performing models (Model D) to improve demand.
-Introduce performance-based incentives for sales representatives to boost productivity, even though current contributions are relatively balanced.

KPI 2: Profit Contribution 
-Prioritize high-margin segments (Petrol vehicles) in the short term to sustain profitability, while optimizing cost structures for Electric Vehicles to improve their margins.
-Review pricing and production costs for low-profit models (Model D) to improve profitability or consider phasing them out if performance does not improve.
-Align sales strategy with profitability by focusing not just on volume, but on high-margin products and regions.

KPI 3: Environmental Sustainability 
-Increase investment in Electric Vehicles (EVs) through marketing, incentives, and awareness campaigns to drive adoption and improve sustainability performance.
-Introduce government or customer incentives (e.g., discounts, financing options) to make EVs more attractive and accessible.
-Develop a balanced strategy that maintains current profitability (from petrol/diesel) while gradually transitioning toward EVs to meet 2030 carbon reduction targets.
